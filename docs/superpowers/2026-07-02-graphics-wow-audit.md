## Baseline diagnosis (what actually drags the image down today)

1. **Zero anti-aliasing anywhere.** `antialias:false` (main.js:159) and a single-sample
   composer RT — every voxel edge, rail line and lantern string crawls. Worst on the slow
   title orbit, where the whole frame is edges in motion.
2. **Distance shimmer.** NearestFilter with `generateMipmaps:false` (textures.js:1118-1120)
   makes every far hillside sparkle with single-pixel noise.
3. **Water is a static blue sheet.** No ripple, no glitter, no depth read, sea stops dead at
   the chunk-stream edge.
4. **Terrain reads as wallpaper.** Identical 16px tiles to the fog line; sun never varies
   across the landscape (no cloud shadows); fog is purely radial so valleys never hold mist.
5. **Night is a void.** Only 6 pooled PointLights in the whole world; no lit windows, no
   ground light, no block light. Plain (the kids' tablets) has essentially no lighting at all.
6. **Fine barely earns its cost.** Verified in preview: at village range, Fine vs Plain is
   hard to tell apart. Bloom/exposure/grade are static constants all day.
7. **Latent bugs found by the audit:** star field uses `Math.random()` (violates invariant 6
   determinism); pixelRatio never re-resolves on monitor/DPR change; held-item viewmodel
   leaks a texture per item swap; shadow camera isn't texel-snapped so shadow edges swim.

## Full proposal digest (38 items, verbatim from the audit with both red-team verdicts)

### [0] Cloud shadows sweeping the moor
lens: lighting-atmosphere | effort: M | wow: 5 | perf_cost: Fine only: ~8 hash calls per terrain fragment inside a coherent uniform branch (comparable to the existing snow wash); zero on Plain (branch skipped), zero draw calls, zero allocations — one uniform write per frame.
WHAT: On partly-cloudy days, soft dark patches glide across the open moor in time with the dome clouds — hillsides dim and brighten as cloud banks pass over. This is THE missing daytime cue on wide moorland: right now the sun never varies across the landscape, so the moor reads as evenly lit plastic. With this, standing on a top watching a cloud shadow race up the dale toward you becomes a real moment.
HOW: Extend the existing terrain shader injection in src/mesher.js (addSnow, lines 21-43 — add a sibling addCloudShadow applied to the same opaque+cutout materials, extending the customProgramCacheKey). Port the dome's hash/noise/fbm (src/sky.js:128-132) into the Lambert fragment at 2 octaves, sampled at world XZ (the vSnowWX/vSnowWZ varyings already exist) scaled ~0.012/block, drifted by the SAME wind vector the dome uses (uTime * vec2(0.012, 0.007) at src/sky.js:139) so ground shadows and sky clouds move together. Inject after '#include <lights_fragment_end>' and multiply reflectedLight.directDiffuse only (cloud blocks direct sun, not ambient — physically right and keeps shade readable). Coverage from a uCloudShadowAmt uniform = domeMat uClouds.value * dayness * (1 - grey saturation), written per frame next to setSnowLevel in src/main.js:~4789; expose the values from sky.update (src/sky.js:388-393). Wrap the whole term in `if (uCloudShadowAmt > 0.001)` so a zero uniform skips the ALU. Exact dome-to-ground registration is not needed — same wind vector + similar feature scale reads as correct. Add 2-3 source assertions to scripts/verify-graphics.mjs (uniform present, Plain sets it 0).
PLAIN: uCloudShadowAmt held at 0 under Plain — the injected branch never executes, terrain shading is byte-identical to today. Also self-zeroes at night and in full overcast on Fine.
FEAS(modify): Sound (lights_fragment_end + reflectedLight.directDiffuse exist in r166 Lambert; varyings verified) — but a material has ONE onBeforeCompile slot, so a 'sibling addCloudShadow' would clobber addSnow's handler: fold the injection into addSnow's handler (or wrap/chain the previous one) and extend the cache key there.
PERF(keep): Uniform-branch-gated 2-octave fbm on terrain fragments, zero on Plain, self-zeroes at night/overcast; same cost class as the shipped snow wash. Claims verified against mesher.js addSnow and sky.js dome noise. Keep the stated 2-octave cap and the >0.001 branch as hard requirements.

### [1] Valley mist — height fog lying in the dales
lens: lighting-atmosphere | effort: M | wow: 5 | perf_cost: ~6 ALU per terrain fragment on both tiers (no new varyings, no textures, no draw calls); one Color lerp + two uniform writes per frame on the CPU. Effectively free — same class of cost as the shipped snow wash.
WHAT: At dawn, dusk, and in misty weather, a luminous mist pools in the valley bottoms: the Esk valley fills with a soft white layer while the moor tops stay clear above it. Stand at altitude and look down on a river of mist; descend into it and the world dims. Distance fog today is purely radial (THREE.Fog), so a valley 40 blocks BELOW you is exactly as clear as a hilltop 40 blocks away — this adds the vertical dimension that makes the real North York Moors photograph the way they do.
HOW: New addMist() injection on all three shared terrain materials in src/mesher.js (same onBeforeCompile pattern as addSnow/addIce, appended into the same cache keys). Inject after '#include <fog_fragment>': `float mist = uMistAmt * (1.0 - exp(-max(0.0, uMistY - vSnowY) * 0.20)) * smoothstep(8.0, 45.0, vFogDepth); gl_FragColor.rgb = mix(gl_FragColor.rgb, uMistCol, clamp(mist, 0.0, 0.9));` — vSnowY (world height) already exists as a varying from the snow injection, and vFogDepth is provided free by Lambert's USE_FOG path in r166. uMistY sits near WATER_LEVEL+6 ≈ 32 (valley floors; moor tops at 45+ rise clear). Drive uMistAmt in sky.update (src/sky.js:369-382, next to the fog-far logic): peak at golden hour x misty/clear-morning weather x autumn season warmth, deterministic from the shared clock and weather state; uMistCol = fog colour lerped toward the current sun colour so dawn mist glows amber. Add a matching horizon whitening band to the dome shader (uMistBand lifting col near dir.y≈0, src/sky.js:133-145) so the mist layer continues visually past the last chunk.
PLAIN: Runs on both tiers (it is a diffuse colour mix, tablet-safe), which is deliberate — Plain currently has almost no atmosphere. A single uMistAmt=0 kill switch restores today's look exactly if Plain ever needs it; verify-graphics asserts the uniform default is 0.
FEAS(modify): vFogDepth is real under USE_FOG and the post-fog_fragment injection point is correct, but (a) a standalone addMist() overwrites the existing onBeforeCompile — fold into addSnow/addIce handlers; (b) vSnowY only exists in the two addSnow materials — the liquid material (addIce) needs its own world-Y varying or should be skipped.
PERF(modify): Shader math is cheap and sound, but ship with uMistAmt forced to 0 on Plain (byte-identical, satisfying invariant 5's spirit that Plain = today's pipeline) and only enable on Plain after a real-tablet frame check. Add the verify assertion for the 0 default as proposed.

### [2] Golden-hour sun shafts (screen-space god rays)
lens: lighting-atmosphere | effort: M | wow: 5 | perf_cost: One fullscreen 16-tap pass, only while enabled (roughly 15% of the day cycle, and only when the camera faces sunward). At pixelRatio 1.5 on integrated GPUs this is ~1-2ms while active; pass.enabled=false the rest of the time is zero.
WHAT: At dawn and dusk under Fine, visible shafts of light radiate from the low sun across the misty moor — trees, cottages, and hillside silhouettes carve dark rays out of the glow. Combined with the valley mist above, a low winter sun streaming down Eskdale becomes the game's signature shot (and the title flyover's winter-dawn vignettes get it for free, since the title renders through the same composer).
HOW: New src/sunshafts.js exporting a ShaderPass (three/addons/postprocessing/ShaderPass.js, r166) inserted in setupComposer (src/main.js:296-308) between the UnrealBloomPass and OutputPass, so it operates in linear HDR pre-tonemap and the grade pass finishes it. Fragment: 16 taps marching from vUv toward uSunNDC with per-tap decay ~0.94, sampling luminance-thresholded colour (HDR sky/sun exceed 1.0, terrain does not — the threshold IS the occlusion mask, no extra depth pass needed), added at uStrength. Per frame in renderFrame: project the existing sun position (sky.sunSprite.position, src/sky.js:299) via Vector3.project(camera) to get uSunNDC; uStrength = goldenFactor (recompute the src/sky.js:283 'golden' term) * clearness * smoothstep facing-the-sun * (1 - offscreen distance falloff); set pass.enabled = uStrength > 0.01 so the pass costs literally nothing outside golden hour or when looking away. Temporal dither the ray start offset by the grade shader's hash12 trick to hide banding at 16 taps. Verify-graphics gains assertions for pass ordering (bloom -> shafts -> OutputPass -> grade).
PLAIN: Fine-only by construction — Plain has no composer (src/main.js:261-265), so the pass never exists there. Today's look untouched.
FEAS(keep): ShaderPass exists in r166, insertion between bloom and OutputPass is valid HDR space, sun projection + enabled-gating are sound; the luma-threshold occlusion mask is approximate (sun sprite is ~1.0, not >1.0 HDR) but workable at this art style. Kill the two duplicates (24, 35) and ship this one.
PERF(modify): Keep this as the ONE god-ray implementation — kill duplicates 24 and 35. Guards: cap at 12 taps on first ship, hard-assert pass.enabled=false outside the golden window in verify-graphics, and have the proposal-28 governor disable the pass entirely once resolution has stepped down. 1-2ms on Iris Xe is only tolerable with those.

### [3] Living golden-hour grade: adaptive exposure, warm split-tone, dusk bloom
lens: lighting-atmosphere | effort: S | wow: 4 | perf_cost: Zero GPU cost (same passes, same taps) — three scalar uniform/property writes per frame. The only risk is tuning time, not runtime.
WHAT: The image itself breathes with the day: exposure opens up as night falls or when you step under a roof (so lantern-lit interiors read instead of crushing to black), the grade warms from neutral noon to amber-gold at the horizon hours, and bloom swells at dusk so lit windows and lanterns across the dale glow. Individually subtle; together they make dawn, noon, dusk, and night feel like four different films rather than one image with the sun moved.
HOW: All in src/main.js. (1) Exposure: replace the constant renderer.toneMappingExposure = 1.25 (line 254) with a per-frame eased target computed from state already in hand — sky dayness/golden, the 'covered' roof flag (main.js:4794-4801), storm-lantern-held: ~1.15 bright midday, 1.3 at golden hour, 1.5 under cover at night; ease at dt*2 for a deliberate eye-adaptation lag. (2) Grade warmth: add uWarmth to GradeShader (lines 110-136) and lerp the two existing split-tone vectors toward a warmer pair (highlights toward 1.07/1.01/0.92) as golden rises — 2 uniform writes in renderFrame (line 335) where uTime is already written. (3) Bloom drive: bloomPass.strength = 0.32 + 0.13 * max(golden, nightness) per frame (property write, threshold stays 0.85). Expose a sky.gradeState() helper returning {golden, nightness, dayness} so main.js reads one source of truth. Update the verify-graphics assertions that pin exposure 1.25 / strength 0.32 to assert the new base values and the drive functions instead.
PLAIN: Entirely inside the Fine-only composer/tone-mapping path; Plain has NoToneMapping and no grade pass, so it is untouched by definition (exposure is ignored under NoToneMapping).
FEAS(keep): All three drives verified feasible (toneMappingExposure per-frame write, GradeShader split-tone vectors exist at main.js:125-126, UnrealBloomPass reads .strength each render at line 265); note verify-graphics doesn't actually pin exposure 1.25 or strength 0.32 — only the 0.85 threshold regex, which survives. Supersedes 25 and 27.
PERF(kill): Pure duplicate bundle: exposure = proposal 25, bloom drive = proposal 27, warmth = proposal 26's uWarmth. The granular S-effort versions are better specified and independently testable. Shipping both double-applies exposure and bloom drive.

### [4] The 1900 night sky: seeded stars with magnitudes, Milky Way, moon phases and halo
lens: lighting-atmosphere | effort: M | wow: 4 | perf_cost: +750 points in one draw call (trivial); ~5 extra dome ALU that fade to zero by day via the uniform; moon redraw is once per game day; halo is one sprite. Safe on tablets.
WHAT: Nights become an event, as they truly were on a pre-electric moor: ~1100 stars with realistic brightness spread and subtle blue-white-to-amber colour, a faint Milky Way band arching across the zenith on clear nights, stars that twinkle gently under Fine, a moon that waxes and wanes on a real 29.5-day cycle with maria blotches, and a soft 22-degree halo ringing it in misty weather ('a ring around the moon means rain soon' — and in this game's weather machine, it literally does, since misty precedes rain). Villagers and folklore quests gain a shared moon calendar to reference.
HOW: All in src/sky.js. (1) Stars: replace the Math.random() field (lines 90-100 — currently violating the determinism invariant) with a mulberry32-seeded build: position + per-vertex aMag (power-law, ~6% bright) + vertex colour (temperature ramp cool-white to amber). Use PointsMaterial with vertexColors plus a small onBeforeCompile replacing gl_PointSize with `size * aMag` and, under Fine, multiplying by a twinkle term hash(position, uStarTime); Plain gets the static field (omit the time uniform). (2) Milky Way: in the existing dome fragment (lines 124-146), add a great-circle band term `exp(-pow(dot(dir, GPOLE), 2.0) * 16.0)` textured by the fbm already compiled there, scaled by a uStarAmt uniform fed from the same starA * (1-grey) value at line 384 — ~5 shader lines, zero new samplers. (3) Moon phases: redraw the 64px moon canvas (lines 76-87) whenever this.day changes — phase = (day % 29.53)/29.53, classic two-arc terminator, 3-4 seeded grey maria; one CanvasTexture.needsUpdate per game day. (4) Halo: one extra sprite sharing a new radial-ring CanvasTexture parented at the moon position, opacity = mistiness * moonUp * (1-grey). Verify: a headless check that two Sky constructions produce identical star buffers (determinism regained).
PLAIN: Plain keeps the improved static field, Milky Way, phases and halo (all cheaper than the existing 2400-flake snow loop) but skips the twinkle uniform — or, if maximal caution wins, Plain pins star count at 350 behind the same gfx flag the star-size swap already uses (src/main.js:283).
FEAS(keep): Confirmed: stars use Math.random() today (real determinism violation), PointsMaterial supports onBeforeCompile and points.glsl.js has the exact 'gl_PointSize = size;' line to replace, dome fbm exists at sky.js:132, mkDisc canvas redraw is trivial.
PERF(keep): Fixes a confirmed live determinism-invariant violation (Math.random star field, sky.js:92-95). +1100 points in one draw call is trivial; moon canvas redraw once per game day; halo one sprite. The 'ring around the moon' weather-sign is period-perfect. This proposal owns the moon halo — see proposal 22.

### [5] Hearthlight: lit windows across the dale and ground light pools
lens: lighting-atmosphere | effort: M | wow: 4 | perf_cost: Worst case ~30 pane meshes + ~40 pool quads = ~70 small draw calls at night within r=48, all sharing 2 materials and 2 geometries; rebuild churn rides the existing throttled overlay pattern. No PointLights added (the Merlin lesson). CPU per frame: one uniform tick.
WHAT: From dusk, cottage windows across every village glow warm amber — and each lit window and placed lantern throws a soft pool of light on the ground beneath it. Households go dark one by one as the night wears on (hashed 'bedtimes'), so a village at 9pm and a village at 2am read differently. Under Fine the panes punch the bloom threshold and the dale becomes a scatter of glowing points from a hilltop; on Plain — which today has only 6 pooled point lights for the entire world — this is the first time light visibly lands on the ground at all.
HOW: New src/hearthLayer.js modelled on the SeasonalLayer windowed-overlay lifecycle (src/seasonalLayer.js:67-89: r=48, 0.4s rebuild throttle, key includes time-bucket).
