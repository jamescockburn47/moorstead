## pipeline

### Fine/Plain tiering
This area IS the tiering mechanism. Fine = ACES tone mapping + exposure 1.25 + PCFSoft 2048px player-following shadow rig + composer stack (bloom/OutputPass/grade+dither) + brighter stars + Fine sky light curves. Plain = the safety net: renderer left at library defaults (NoToneMapping, exposure 1, shadows off), composer disposed (src/main.js:265), direct renderer.render path — and scripts/verify-graphics.mjs:100-108 asserts source-level that the Plain path stays byte-identical (original sun/ambient/colour curves, composer torn down, direct render intact). resolveQuality (src/sky.js:24): explicit stored choice always wins; else Fine on desktop, Plain when touch is primary. Toggle is live from the pause menu with a full material-recompile traverse (src/main.js:830-839, 286-290); applyQuality re-runs on every new world (src/main.js:561). Any pipeline change must keep or update the ~30 source-string assertions in verify-graphics.mjs (ACES, PCFSoft, 2048 map, ±70 frustum, bloom threshold 0.85, OutputPass ordering, vignette+dither, Plain untouched).

### Perf facts
pixelRatio capped 1.5, antialias off — base fill cost contained. Fine adds per frame: one full shadow pass (2048^2 re-rendered EVERY frame — renderer.shadowMap.autoUpdate is never throttled), UnrealBloom's internal 5-level separable mip blur chain, plus OutputPass and grade pass fullscreen quads; EffectComposer default RT is HalfFloat (r166) so ~3 extra fullscreen targets live. Light budget: up to 8 point lights (6-pool lanterns + torch + storm lantern) + 1 directional + 1 ambient; none cast shadows. applyShadowFlags does a whole-scene traverse every 0.6 s (src/main.js:5235-5238) — cost scales with scene-graph size, not per-frame but recurring; quality flips traverse ALL materials forcing shader recompiles (an intentional hitch). Per-frame allocations in this area are small: updateLanterns builds and sorts a near[] array each frame (src/main.js:5245-5251); held-item swap allocates a new TextureLoader + texture and never disposes the old map (src/main.js:4996-5000, minor leak); renderFrame itself is allocation-free. Camera far 600 but fog.far runs 22-160, so overdraw is fog-hidden rather than culled by camera range. Title screen runs the full Fine pipeline behind the menu (same cost as gameplay). Resize re-sizes renderer + composer but never re-reads devicePixelRatio. dt clamped at 0.05; grade uTime wraps mod 64 to avoid float drift.

### Headroom noted by the surveyor
- **Anti-aliasing under Fine (FXAA pass or MSAA composer target)** [S, wow 4]: Fine currently has ZERO AA: renderer is antialias:false (src/main.js:159) and the EffectComposer render target is single-sample, so voxel edges, rails, and lantern strings crawl — the single most visible pipeline defect. Cheapest: append the FXAA ShaderPass from three/addons/shaders/FXAAShader.js after the grade pass in setupComposer (one pass, integrated-GPU friendly, resolution uniform updated in the existing resize handler). Alternative on WebGL2: construct EffectComposer with a WebGLRenderTarget({samples:4, type:HalfFloatType}) for true MSAA. Plain path untouched; verify-graphics gains one source assertion.
- **Texel-snap the player-following shadow camera** [S, wow 3]: The ±70-block ortho shadow frustum rides playerPos continuously (src/sky.js:278-282), so every step makes shadow edges shimmer and crawl — classic un-snapped-cascade artifact. Quantise the shadow camera position to whole shadow-map texels (140/2048 ≈ 0.068 world units) along its right/up axes before updating. A few lines in the sky update or applyQuality; removes the most visible Fine shadow artifact for free.
- **Golden-hour sun shafts (radial post pass)** [M, wow 4]: A Fine-only half-res radial-blur ShaderPass: 16 taps sampled toward the sun's screen position (the pipeline already computes sunSprite world position, src/sky.js:299; project to NDC per frame), additively composited, gated by the existing 'golden' low-sun factor (src/sky.js:283) and faded out when the sun leaves screen. Low winter light streaming across a misty moor is exactly the period mood; deterministic, procedural, skipped entirely on Plain (no composer). Cost ≈ one half-res fullscreen pass, only active near dawn/dusk.
- **Film grain + corner chromatic fringe in the existing GradeShader** [S, wow 2]: GradeShader already has hash12() and uTime (src/main.js:120, 133). Add ~6 lines: luminance-scaled monochrome grain (stronger in shadows, ~1.5%) and a subtle warm/cool channel offset scaled by dot(q,q) so it only appears at extreme corners — finishing the 'Victorian hand-tinted plate' identity the shader comments already claim. Zero new passes, zero new uniforms beyond what exists.
- **Second far shadow cascade for the vistas** [L, wow 4]: Beyond 70 blocks the moor is shadowless — far hillsides go flat exactly when the long-shadow dawn/dusk rig (src/main.js:277) is at its best. Hand-roll a second directional light with a 1024px ±300-block ortho shadow map, intensity ~0, shadow-only contribution via a small onBeforeCompile tweak OR use three/addons CSM (needs csm.setupMaterial patching on the Lambert set). Update the far map every ~10 frames (shadowMap.autoUpdate=false + needsUpdate cadence) to keep integrated GPUs safe. Plain unaffected (shadows already off).
- **Throttle shadow-map re-render to buy the AA/shafts budget** [S, wow 1]: The 2048px shadow pass re-renders every frame even when the sun has barely moved. Set renderer.shadowMap.autoUpdate=false and flag needsUpdate every 2nd-3rd frame or when playerPos/sun angle move past a small epsilon (state already in hand in sky.update). Combined with texel-snapping this is visually free and reclaims the single biggest Fine GPU cost — headroom that funds FXAA or sun shafts on integrated GPUs.
- **Frame-time-driven dynamic resolution guard** [M, wow 2]: Keep an EMA of dt in frame() (dt already computed, src/main.js:4544); if it sits above ~26 ms for a few seconds under Fine, step pixelRatio 1.5→1.25→1.0 (renderer.setPixelRatio + composer.setSize), restore when headroom returns, and only suggest the Plain toggle as the last step. Lets stored-Fine users on weak kids' tablets keep shadows and bloom instead of a slideshow — raises the effective visual floor. Headless-safe (pure logic, testable in verify).
- **Deterministic exposure adaptation (eye adaptation without readback)** [M, wow 3]: toneMappingExposure is a constant 1.25 (src/main.js:254). Drive it each frame from state the pipeline already computes — sky.time/dayness, the 'covered' roof flag (src/main.js:4794-4801), and storm-lantern-held — easing between ~1.15 (bright midday) and ~1.5 (under cover at night, so lantern light blooms and interiors read). Pure function of existing state: deterministic, no GPU luminance readback, one uniform write, Plain untouched (exposure ignored under NoToneMapping). Sells the lantern-in-the-dark and stepping-out-at-dawn moments.
- **Dusk-warm bloom drive** [S, wow 2]: bloomPass.strength is a constant 0.32 (src/main.js:303). Modulate it per frame (e.g. 0.32 → 0.45 as the 'golden' low-sun factor rises and at night when lit windows/lanterns are the only sources), one property write in frame() or sky.update. Amber evening glow on lit windows across the dale for essentially zero cost.

## sky (src/sky.js — day/night cycle, sky dome, clouds, stars, precipitation, fog, dread/storm grading, quality light rigs)

### Techniques in use
- Single DirectionalLight doubling as sun AND moon under Fine — swaps position/colour/intensity when sunY<-0.02, cool blue 0x9cbcf0 moonlight, so one shadow rig follows whichever lamp is up (src/sky.js:269-289)
- Piecewise day/night light curves: dayness = clamp((sunY+0.12)*3), Fine runs hotter to compensate ACES mids; golden-hour HSL ramp on sun colour near horizon (src/sky.js:264, 283-285)
- Plain light rig kept as legacy fallback: fixed HSL sun, lower ambient floor, sun position not player-height-relative (src/sky.js:290-296)
- Sky palette keyframe lerp night/dawn/day/dusk keyed on sunY and sunX sign (dawn vs dusk) (src/sky.js:38-43, 303-307)
- Sky dome: BackSide SphereGeometry(500,24,16) ShaderMaterial, horizon→zenith gradient via pow(dir.y, 0.7), bottomColor fed the live computed sky tint each frame, zenith deepened by dayness (src/sky.js:108-151, 365-367)
- Procedural clouds inside the dome fragment shader: 4-octave value-noise fbm on planar projection dir.xz/dir.y, wind drift via uTime, coverage smoothstep driven by weather grey (uClouds eased at dt*0.5), cloud colour lerped dark-to-white by dayness (src/sky.js:128-144, 388-393)
- Sun/moon discs: 64px canvas-circle CanvasTexture sprites repositioned each frame opposite each other (src/sky.js:76-87, 299-300)
- Stars: 350-point THREE.Points hemisphere (r=180), opacity ramps with -sunY, dimmed by overcast grey; Fine boosts opacity x1.6 and point size 1.9 vs 1.4 (src/sky.js:90-100, 384-386; src/main.js:283)
- Linear scene fog with dynamic far per weather (clear 160 / misty >=78 / rain 90 / fog <=28), eased at dt*0.5, and near-ratio that tightens the fade band in thick fog so it truly hides; fog colour copies live sky (src/sky.js:370-382, 361)
- Weather state machine (clear/misty/rain/fog) with random timer, overridden by live Open-Meteo feed (currentWeather) which parks the timer; weather-change toast messages (src/sky.js:225-259)
- Rain: 900 Points, 4x16 canvas linear-gradient streak texture, CPU per-particle fall+recycle loop with needsUpdate, anchored to camera, opacity zeroed under a roof (src/sky.js:154-174, 396-412)
- Snow: 2400 Points, 8px radial-gradient flake texture, slow fall with per-flake sinusoidal wind drift, tall -28..+20 column centred on viewer so flakes fill aerial title orbit too (src/sky.js:177-197, 414-430)
- Lightning flash: scalar spiked to 1 by storm controller, decays over ~220ms, squared easing, floods sun+ambient intensity (flashLift) — light-only, no bolt geometry or dome brightening (src/sky.js:57, 267-268, 279-294; src/storm.js:139)
- Dread (Dracula) grading: sky lerps toward bruise purple 0x1a1020 + blood 0x301018, fog far clamped down, sun/ambient dimmed 35%/25% (src/sky.js:322-327, 374, 279-294)
- The Great Fog: shared-clock (Date.now) whiteout every 3 game days for ~6 game hours, 25s ease, geography-gated by moorGate (eased), whitens sky and collapses fog far to ~7m (src/sky.js:333-344, 375)
- Seasonal cast: warm/cool HSL tint lerped into sky scaled by |warmth|*dayness, plus small ambient/sun intensity multipliers (src/sky.js:347-353)
- Fine night lift: sky lerped toward 0x18233a by nightness so silhouettes and horizon read on the moonlit moor (src/sky.js:354-359)
- Overcast greying: overcastGrey(weather, snow, rain) lerps sky toward grey scaled by dayness (src/sky.js:320-321; src/snow.js)
- lanternFlicker: deterministic two-summed-slow-sines + fast shimmer, bounded [0.9,1.1] — drives held lantern and the storm boss light (src/sky.js:32-36; src/main.js:5017)
- Fine-only sun shadow rig (owned by main but on the sky's sun): 2048px PCFSoft, +/-70m ortho frustum following camera, bias -0.0004 / normalBias 0.8 (src/main.js:270-282)
- Draw-order hygiene: dome renderOrder -1 + frustumCulled false + depthWrite false; sprites/points depthWrite false, fog:false on sky elements (src/sky.js:81, 149-150, 173, 196)

### Fine/Plain tiering
sky.gfx is stamped by Game.applyQuality (src/main.js:267-268); resolveQuality (src/sky.js:24-27) defaults Fine on desktop, Plain when touch is primary, stored localStorage 'moorcraft-gfx' choice wins. FINE differences in this area: (1) ACES-retuned hotter light curves; (2) the single directional becomes a real moon at night (cool blue, raised ambient floor for moonlight navigation); (3) sun positions ride relative to player height so a low sun casts long dawn/dusk shadows; (4) night sky lifted toward 0x18233a; (5) stars 1.6x brighter and size 1.9; (6) sun.castShadow with 2048 PCFSoft ortho rig; plus renderer-side ACES tone mapping exposure 1.25 and the composer (UnrealBloom 0.32/0.5/0.85 + GradeShader vignette/grade/dither) that blooms the sun disc and horizon (src/main.js:248-308). PLAIN keeps today's legacy pipeline untouched: flat HSL sun, no moon light, no shadows, no post. Everything else in sky.js is UNTIERED — dome cloud shader, star count (350), rain (900) and snow (2400) particle counts, fog logic, dread/Great Fog all run identically on both tiers.

### Perf facts
6 draw calls total from this module (dome, stars, rain, snow, 2 sprites). Lights: 1 DirectionalLight + 1 AmbientLight; one 2048^2 PCFSoft shadow map under Fine only. Particles: rain 900 / snow 2400, both CPU-simulated — per-particle JS loops with position needsUpdate upload every frame whenever amount > 0.02 (snow loop = 2400 Math.sin calls/frame, src/sky.js:398-430); counts are NOT tiered down on Plain/tablets. Dome is SphereGeometry(500,24,16) (~768 tris) but the fragment shader runs 4-octave fbm (16 hash calls/px) over most of the frame every frame, and with renderOrder=-1 + depthWrite:false it is drawn FIRST so terrain overdraws it — full-screen fbm cost is paid even where the sky is hidden (src/sky.js:108-151). Textures: 3 tiny CanvasTextures (2x 64^2 discs, 4x16 rain streak, 8^2 flake) — negligible. Per-frame heap churn: ~8-12 `new THREE.Color`/`.clone()` allocations per update (src/sky.js:321, 325-326, 342, 349-350, 358, 367, 393). Determinism caveat: star positions, weather fallback machine, and particle respawns all use Math.random(), not the world seed (src/sky.js:92-94, 243-258).

### Headroom noted by the surveyor
- **Sun-aware dome: forward-scatter glow + sunlit cloud edges** [M, wow 4]: Pass a sunDir uniform into the existing dome shader (src/sky.js:108-147). Add (a) a Mie-ish pow(dot(dir,sunDir)) warm glow around the sun that swells at golden hour, (b) a dawn/dusk horizon band on the sun side, and (c) silver-lined clouds: shade the existing fbm cloud term by dot(dir,sunDir) so edges facing the sun brighten and bases opposite darken. Clouds are currently flat-lit by dayness only (src/sky.js:143, 393). Deterministic, ~10 extra ALU lines, degrades to nothing by zeroing the uniform on Plain. Bloom under Fine would pick up the glow for free.
- **Seeded star field with magnitudes, colour variance and Milky Way band** [M, wow 4]: Stars are 350 uniform white points placed with Math.random() (src/sky.js:92-94) — breaking the determinism-from-seed invariant. Rebuild with a mulberry32-style seeded RNG: ~900 stars with per-vertex size/colour attributes (blue-white to amber, power-law magnitudes) via a small PointsMaterial onBeforeCompile or vertex colours, plus a clustered band across the dome for the Milky Way — dead right for a pre-electric-light 1900 moor where the night sky would be spectacular. Fine gets a slow twinkle (size jitter from a time uniform); Plain gets the static field. Fixes an invariant violation and is the cheapest large night-sky win.
- **Procedural lightning bolt + cloud illumination on flash** [M, wow 4]: sky.flash currently only lifts light intensity (src/sky.js:267-268) — there is no bolt and the clouds stay dark. On spike, generate a seeded jagged polyline (6-10 segments, 1-2 branches) as a LineSegments mesh from cloud base to horizon at a random-but-seeded bearing, visible ~2 frames, and add a uFlash uniform to the dome shader that whitens the cloud term so the whole sky blinks. Storm already owns the timing (src/storm.js:139). Plain shows the same bolt (one line draw call, trivial); the Dracula storm boss fight is exactly where kids are looking up.
- **Two-layer clouds: high cirrus + low cumulus with darkened bases** [S, wow 3]: Add a second fbm sample in the dome shader at ~3x scale drifting at a different wind vector for thin high cirrus, and darken the existing cumulus by its own density (mix cloudCol toward a grey-base colour by n) so overcast reads volumetric instead of a flat stencil. ~6 shader lines reusing the existing fbm (src/sky.js:132-143). On Plain, optionally drop the fbm loop from 4 to 3 octaves to pay for it — net-neutral cost.
- **Moon phases from the day counter** [S, wow 3]: The moon is a flat 64px canvas disc (src/sky.js:86-87). Redraw the canvas whenever this.day changes: phase = (day % 29.53)/29.53, draw the lit limb with two arcs (classic terminator construction) plus 3-4 seeded grey maria blotches. Zero per-frame cost, one CanvasTexture update per game day, works identically on Plain, and gives the shared world a moon calendar villagers/folklore quests could reference — deeply period-apt for 1900.
- **GPU-side precipitation: kill the CPU loops, triple the snow** [M, wow 3]: Move rain/snow fall to the vertex shader: keep static base positions, compute y as mod(baseY - uTime*speed, span) and x-drift as sin, via onBeforeCompile on the existing PointsMaterials. Deletes the 900+2400 per-particle JS loops and per-frame attribute uploads (src/sky.js:398-430) — the single biggest CPU cost in this file on kids' tablets — and the freed budget lets Fine run 6000 flakes / slanted wind-blown rain while Plain keeps current counts at near-zero CPU.
- **Dawn-glow fog: tint fog and horizon toward the sun colour at low sun** [S, wow 3]: Fog colour is a straight copy of the sky tint (src/sky.js:361). Lerp scene.fog.color (and dome bottomColor) toward the current sun colour by golden*clearness*(sun-side factor) so misty mornings glow amber and dusk haze warms — one Color.lerp per frame, no shader work, identical on Plain. Cheapest atmosphere-per-line-of-code in the file.
- **22-degree moon halo in misty weather** [S, wow 3]: One extra sprite: a canvas radial ring texture (transparent centre, soft ring) parented at the moon sprite position, opacity = mistiness * moonUp * (1-grey). Victorian weather-lore made flesh — 'a ring around the moon means rain soon' — and it foreshadows the weather machine's actual behaviour since misty often precedes rain (src/sky.js:245). One draw call, Plain-safe, S effort.
- **Rare seeded aurora event on the shared clock** [M, wow 5]: Following the Great Fog pattern (shared Date.now cycle, src/sky.js:333-344): once every ~10 game days at night, draw 2-3 aurora curtains in the dome shader — vertical fbm bands (reuse existing fbm) in green/faint red, masked to the northern sky (dir.z sign), swaying with uTime. Auroras were genuinely recorded over Yorkshire c.1900 (Carrington-era folklore still fresh). Shared-clock = every player sees it together, like the fog and the train. Fine-only uniform toggle; Plain skips the branch.
- **Hoist per-frame Color allocations** [S, wow 1]: update() allocates ~8-12 THREE.Color per frame via new/clone (src/sky.js:321, 325-326, 342, 349-350, 358, 367, 393). Hoist module-level scratch Colors. No visual change; GC-hitch hygiene for tablets that also buys headroom for the ideas above.

## meshing-textures (src/mesher.js, src/textures.js)

### Techniques in use
- Culled-face (NOT greedy) per-block voxel meshing: one quad per visible face, neighbour-occlusion skip via isOpaque — src/mesher.js:132-243 (skip at 219-220); no quad merging anywhere
- Classic 4-level per-vertex AO (side1/side2/corner rule) baked into the vertex-color attribute — src/mesher.js:83 (AO_LEVELS [0.42,0.62,0.8,1.0]), 223-238
- AO-aware quad flip to kill anisotropy artefacts — src/mesher.js:110-114
- Baked directional face lighting multiplied into vertex color (FACE_LIGHT [0.78,0.78,0.55,1.0,0.68,0.68]; this IS the lighting model on Plain) — src/mesher.js:84,106-107
- Exactly 3 shared MeshLambertMaterials for all terrain: opaque (atlas+vertexColors), cutout (alphaTest .5, DoubleSide), liquid (transparent .78, depthWrite false, DoubleSide, renderOrder 2) — src/mesher.js:59-70, 254
- onBeforeCompile snow-wash injection: uSnowLine/uSnowAmt uniforms + per-vertex aSnowExp sky-exposure attribute + world-pos drift sine + up-normal smoothstep; uniform-driven per frame, zero re-mesh — src/mesher.js:21-43, driven at src/main.js:4789
- Per-column sky-exposure scan (Int16Array skyTop) so snow only lands on exterior top faces, not cave ceilings — src/mesher.js:139-146, applied at 239
- onBeforeCompile ice tint on liquid: uFrozen uniform x aFreeze attribute, coast-gated via freezableWater — src/mesher.js:45-57, 210-211
- Time-animated glint sparkle compiled into the shared cutout material (uGlintTime; aGlint attribute is fed by floraLayer.js:35 / festivalKit.js:63 meshes sharing the material; chunk cutout geometry omits it so defaults to 0) — src/mesher.js:31-38
- Cross-quad billboard flora with deterministic per-block yaw/jitter/half-width/height from hash2i(wx,wz,n) — src/mesher.js:177-194; structural cutouts (torch/signpost/flag) stay centred+upright — src/mesher.js:168-176
- Liquid pass: 0.12 top-surface drop, faces only against non-liquid/non-opaque, no interior faces — src/mesher.js:198-213

# Part 2 — grounding investigations (dynamic water & air)

## River / sea / coast data model in Moorstead worldgen (src/moorsgeo.js, src/geography.js, src/worldgen.js, src/mesher.js, src/defs.js)

### Findings
- GLOBAL: WATER_LEVEL = 26 (src/defs.js:5). B.WATER is kind 'liquid', unbreakable, not placeable (src/defs.js:129, 197, 351). B.BOG is a second liquid (defs.js:130), stylised world only (worldgen.js:345-349).
- RIVERS EXIST ONLY IN THE MOORS (realWorld) WORLD: source data is data/moors-data.json rivers[] — 10 named rivers, each {name, dale, size:'major'|'beck', points:[[x,z],...]} ordered polylines (Esk 436 pts; verified via node). The stylised world (src/geography.js) has NO rivers at all — only the analytic coast (coastX/coastT, geography.js:55-65) and bog pools.
- RIVER CARVE PIPELINE (src/moorsgeo.js): constants at :30-37 (RIVER_HALF major=3/beck=2 half-width, SNAP=5, FREEBOARD=1, RESAMPLE=4, SMOOTH_WIN=2, TAPER=60, MAXCUT=4, BED_DEPTH=2). _riverProfile() at moorsgeo.js:816-858 resamples each polyline to even 4-block chainage giving [{x,z,s}] with cumulative chainage s (_resample :802-812), anchors a per-sample valley floor as the perpendicular min over ±SNAP blocks (:820-826), smooths it (:827-830), and REVERSES the polyline if it runs uphill (:831) — so after profiling, POINT ORDER IS ALWAYS SOURCE→MOUTH. Flow direction is therefore derivable BOTH from point order (res[i]→res[i+1]) and from the water-level gradient.
- RIVERS STEP DOWN: wl[i] = max(WATER_LEVEL, min(wl[i-1], sfloor[i]-FREEBOARD)) at moorsgeo.js:834-835 — the water surface is a MONOTONE NON-INCREASING staircase downstream, clamped never below sea level, so the lower reach sits at WATER_LEVEL and meets the sea flush. Per-column wl is Math.round of the interpolated profile (moorsgeo.js:876), so natural 1-block weir/waterfall steps occur wherever the smoothed valley floor drops a block; the smoothing + monotone clamp means steps are typically 1 block (gentle weirs, not big falls), and the carve is capped at MAXCUT=4 below local ground (no canyons, :880-881). A mouth extension (:838-851) appends samples toward the nearest coastT>0 cell at wl=WATER_LEVEL so every river genuinely reaches the sea.
- riverColumn(x,z) at moorsgeo.js:863-884 returns {bed, wl, dale} for columns within half-width of the centreline: it computes the nearest segment index i, projection t and chainage s (:868-877) but DISCARDS the tangent — the downstream flow direction is one line away from being exposed. It returns null where coastT>0 (:875) so the river never trenches the sea bed. riverWaterLevel(x,z) → wl|null (:887); nearRiver(x,z,pad) (:890-897). Per-call cost: bbox pre-filter per river then a LINEAR scan of all resampled segments (no spatial cell index — cf. railpath.js:260-265 which has one).
- THE CARVE HAPPENS CLIENT-SIDE IN CHUNK GEN, NOT IN heightRaw (moorsgeo.js:344-345 comment; worldgen.js:330-342): generateChunk clears the channel to AIR from rc.bed+1 up to un-carved ground h+2 (worldgen.js:338), lays a GRAVEL bed at rc.bed (:339), and fills B.WATER from bed+1 to rc.wl (:340). Since rc.wl ≥ WATER_LEVEL always, RIVER WATER ABOVE y=26 IS UNAMBIGUOUSLY RIVER; y==26 water can be sea, estuary or lowest reach.
- SEA: filled at worldgen.js:326-328 — wherever ground h < WATER_LEVEL, water fills h+1..WATER_LEVEL (one flat level; the sea is all at y≤26). Moors coastT (moorsgeo.js:60-64) is DEPTH-based, not distance-based: 0 if DEM base ≥ WATER_LEVEL, else smoothstep((26-base)/8) — i.e. a depth-below-waterline proxy (0 at shore, 1 where seabed is 8+ below WL). In the moors world any flooded inland depression also gets coastT>0, so tarn≡sea by every existing field. Stylised-world coastT is analytic in x (geography.js:55-65, Robin Hood's Bay bite), so shoreline normal there is analytic.
- PER-COLUMN COAST DATA THAT EXISTS: coastT (depth proxy, moorsgeo.js:60-64); coastDistCoarse — an 8-block-grid BFS distance field, but seeded FROM sea cells and measuring LAND→sea distance (sea cells are 0) (moorsgeo.js:69-93); _coastDist — fine Manhattan-ring search from a land column to the nearest sea column, capped (:97-106); coastCliffTop — inverse-distance blend of 8 named coastalCliffs anchors (Whitby 6, Boulby 13 etc.) (:128-139 + data). NO stored coast orientation/normal and NO sea-side distance-to-land field — but the codebase already derives the coast normal by finite-differencing the distance field (moorsgeo.js:182 in _coastHug: gx = cd(x+4,z)-cd(x-4,z) = inland gradient).
- SEA VS RIVER IS ALREADY DISTINGUISHED PER-VERTEX AT MESH TIME: the liquid pass in buildChunkMeshes calls world.gen.geo.coastT(x,z) per water face and bakes an aFreeze float attribute via freezableWater (mesher.js:198-212, esp. :210; snow.js:59-63: BOG always freezable, WATER freezable iff coastT ≤ 0.15 — the open sea never freezes). So the mesher already reaches the geo object per column during meshing — precedent for baking more.
- MESH-TIME PLUMBING PRECEDENT FOR NEW ATTRIBUTES: GeoBuilder already carries two custom per-vertex floats (exp→aSnowExp, frz→aFreeze; mesher.js:95, 96-116, 124-125), consumed by onBeforeCompile GLSL injections on MeshLambertMaterial (addSnow :21-43, addIce :47-57); there is a live global uniform pattern for animation time (uGlintTime, mesher.js:14-15). Liquid material: transparent 0.78, depthWrite:false, DoubleSide (:64-67); water top face inset 0.12 below block top (:200, 207); liquid renderOrder 2 (:254). Side faces of a water block are emitted when the neighbour is neither the same liquid nor opaque (:203-205) — so every 1-block wl step already renders a vertical water quad (the natural waterfall/weir face to target).
- RAIL×WATER: railpath.js:245-256 lifts the rail deck to riverWaterLevel+4 clearance away from stations, re-ramped at 1-in-8. worldgen.js:438-453 renders crossings: bridgeZone (nearRiver pad 3) → open arch with stone deck slab (:441-443); overSea (coastT>0.5) → sea viaduct (Larpool over the Esk estuary): open span from WATER_LEVEL+1 to deck-2, masonry pier to the seabed every (x+z)%6==0 (:444-449); dressed-stone abutments elsewhere in the zone (:450-451). The culvert keeps the water column continuous under the rails (:340 comment).
- ROADS FORD, NEVER BRIDGE: roads.js:9-10 'Where the lane meets a beck the tiles stop at the bank (NPCs ford it on foot)… No plank bridges'; roads.js:119-123 skips any riverColumn column. roadpath.js:7 confirms no vertical profile or spans are baked. So fords = gaps in the lane tiles at the beck (a natural site for stepping-stone/ripple effects).
- WHITBY HARBOUR (moors world): there is no stamped pier — pierHead() returns the off-map sentinel (moorsgeo.js:742); the harbour IS the DEM Esk estuary, with the Esk's mouth extension guaranteeing a flush river→sea junction at Whitby (Esk last point [1842,3065]); whitbyHarbour() strand point (1828,3046) at moorsgeo.js:762-766. The stylised world's plank pier is stamped at worldgen.js:1367-1383. The coast cross-section (beach 6 wide at WL+1 sand, 2:1 climbable cliff terrace, level clifftop, inland taper) is moorsgeo.js:306-324, and it deliberately leaves a 5-block river corridor uncliffed (_nearRiverData :110-124) so estuaries stay open.

- WINTER: setFrozen tints liquid via aFreeze (mesher.js:45-57); frozen when season.warmth < -0.4 (snow.js:64). Inland becks/tarns freeze, the sea does not — wave/flow animation should gate off against the same uFrozen×aFreeze product.

### Capabilities assessment
- aFlow (river flow direction xz + chainage phase): FULLY SUPPORTED with a one-line API extension — riverColumn already computes the nearest downstream-ordered segment (res[i]→res[i+1]), projection t and chainage s (moorsgeo.js:868-877) and throws the tangent away. Returning {tx,tz,s} (tangent normalised; guaranteed downstream by the reverse at :831) gives worldgen/mesher everything needed at zero extra sampling cost. Missing: no cell index on river segments (linear scan per call) — fine at current scale since worldgen already pays it per column, but an 8-block cell map (copy railpath.js:260-265) makes it O(1) if per-vertex sampling multiplies calls.
- aDrop (bed/surface step for waterfalls-weirs): SUPPORTED — the wl staircase is monotone downstream (moorsgeo.js:834-835) and per-column wl is rounded (:876), so a mesh-time pass can bake max(wl(here) − wl(4-neighbours)) by 4 extra riverWaterLevel calls per water column, or bake the analytic slope (r.wl[i+1]−r.wl[i])/RESAMPLE straight from the profile. The vertical water quad at each step already exists in the liquid mesh (mesher.js:203-207) to attach foam/animation to. Steps are ~1 block (smoothed, MAXCUT-capped) — expect weirs, not Niagara.
- aShore (water-vertex distance to nearest land): PARTIALLY SUPPORTED — for rivers, distance-to-bank falls out of riverColumn (half − best.d). For the SEA it is MISSING today: coastT is a depth proxy (moorsgeo.js:60-64), and the existing BFS field (_coastField :69-93) measures land→sea, with all sea cells at 0. Needed: a second BFS seeded from land cells (base ≥ WATER_LEVEL) over the same 8-block grid — same code shape, one-time cost, gives sea→shore distance for shoreline wave attenuation; refine near shore with an inverted _coastDist ring search. Coast orientation/normal is likewise not stored but cheaply derivable by finite-differencing that field (pattern already in _coastHug, moorsgeo.js:182).
- Sea/river/bog classification per vertex: ALREADY HALF-BUILT — the liquid pass bakes aFreeze from coastT at mesh time (mesher.js:210, snow.js:59-63), proving geo access during meshing is acceptable; water above y=26 is river by construction, y==26 splits by coastT/riverColumn. Estuary blend band = riverColumn(x,z)==null && coastT small, guaranteed flush by the mouth extension (moorsgeo.js:838-851).
- Constraints any water-dynamics effect must respect: (1) liquid chunks are static Lambert meshes — animate via shader-time uniforms (uGlintTime pattern, mesher.js:14-15), never per-frame remesh; (2) coexist with the ice overlay (uFrozen×aFreeze) so frozen becks stop moving; (3) the moors sea-plane backdrop at WATER_LEVEL+0.9 (main.js:3436-3455) and coble/boat anchors at WATER_LEVEL+0.4 assume a flat sea surface; (4) attributes baked from geo are static worldgen truth and won't track player edits — acceptable since water is neither placeable nor breakable (defs.js:129, 351); (5) determinism/additive-protocol invariants (docs/INVARIANTS.md) — all proposed inputs are already deterministic functions of the data JSON; (6) the stylised world has no rivers, so river attributes must degrade to no-ops there (geo.riverColumn guarded with typeof checks throughout, e.g. worldgen.js:334, roads.js:120).
- Not supported without new data: sea currents/flow (no directional data for open water — only shore distance/normal derivable); river width variation beyond the two size classes (half-width 3/2, moorsgeo.js:30); water depth beyond wl−bed ≤ ~4 per column; and any distance-to-coast for sea cells beyond 8-block BFS granularity without the fine inverted ring search.

## Weather / season / heat state available to drive wetness + thermal effects (Moorstead client)

### Findings
- PER-FRAME SCALARS — rain: sky.rainAmount [0,1] eased toward targetRain at rate dt*0.8 (src/sky.js:54, src/sky.js:396); target comes from winterPrecip(season, liveRain, deterministic-snow-fallback) at src/sky.js:309, splitting precip into snow vs rain by season.warmth<0 (src/snow.js:50-55). Live amount = precipitation mm/h / 4, clamped [0,1] (src/weather-live.js:36).
- PER-FRAME SCALARS — snowfall + accumulation: snowFall [0,1] per frame (src/sky.js:309) from live precip or the deterministic shared-clock snowfallIntensity(Date.now(), season) = frost envelope * (0.45 + 0.55*showerOscillation) (src/snow.js:34-39, oscillation is value-noise on wall-clock seconds, src/snow.js:27-30). Asymmetry precedent for 'soak fast / dry slow' already exists in both stepAccumulation's split rates (src/snow.js:20-21) and the player-wetness rates (src/main.js:4816-4817). INVARIANTS rule 6 applies: seed/step from shared inputs, no Math.random for anything cross-client (docs/INVARIANTS.md:64-71).
- LIVE FEED vs SEASON CLOCK (Q4): they own different axes. Sampling — startLiveWeather() (called once at boot, src/main.js:227) fetches every 15 min (TTL, src/weather-live.js:16, 64-67) but indexes the current UTC hour of a 1-day hourly Goathland forecast (src/weather-live.js:49), so effective weather changes at most hourly and all clients agree. Who wins — the live feed wins the weather STATE and precip AMOUNT: when currentWeather() returns a sample, sky.update sets this.weather from live.state and parks the random machine (weatherTimer=1e9, src/sky.js:226-238). The season clock wins the FORM and everything ground-truth-thermal: winterPrecip converts live precip to snow whenever season.warmth<0 (src/snow.js:50-55); season alone drives snowAccum/snow-line/freezing (src/main.js:4788-4790), the seasonal sky tint (src/sky.js:347-353), atlas retint (src/main.js:4792-4793), and the offline deterministic snowfall fallback (src/snow.js:34-39). So real-world July rain over Goathland falls as snow in game-winter. Failure mode: any fetch fault leaves cached as-is (null first time, stale thereafter — never expired, src/weather-live.js:57-59), and sky falls back to its per-client random machine (src/sky.js:242-259). overcastGrey lets a clear live forecast read sunny even in deep game-winter (src/snow.js:66-71).

### Capabilities assessment
- Heat-shimmer effect: fully drivable today from existing per-frame state — a shimmer01 = f(season.warmth, weather==='clear' or 1-grey, dayness or |time-0.5|, rainAmount≈0, optionally altitude) computed where sky/season are both in hand (the block at src/main.js:4772-4830, or inside sky.update which already receives season). Deterministic/shared while live weather has a sample; degrades to per-client during feed outages. No heat model exists on the player side (temperatureTarget caps at 20 for warmth>=0), so a gameplay heat effect (not just visual) would need a new branch in src/temperature.js.
- Dew-at-dawn effect: time-of-day and season inputs exist (dawn band, frost, greenness), and 'clear right now' exists — but 'clear overnight' history is missing. Either accept the current-state proxy (clear+dry at dawn) or add a tiny overnight clear-sky accumulator following the snowAccum pattern; hourly live sampling makes the proxy fairly honest.
- Ground wetness ease (soak fast / dry slow): entirely new state, but the codebase has a ready-made template — pure step function in a snow.js-style module, Game-owned scalar seeded at load and stepped next to snowAccum (src/main.js:4788), never persisted, surfaced to terrain via a mesher.js uniform like setSnowLevel; inputs (rainAmount, snowFall melt, season.warmth for dry rate, dayness for sun-drying) all exist per frame. Missing pieces: any per-location moisture variation (the snow idiom is a single global scalar + shader noise for spatial variation, src/mesher.js:30), and offline non-winter rain is always 0, so ground wetness would be live-feed-driven outside winter.
- Wind-driven effects: a real [0,1] wind scalar from Open-Meteo already exists (weather-live.js:38) but is dropped on the floor — sky.js would need one line to cache live.windiness alongside liveRain/liveFog to make it available; until then only the altitude proxy at main.js:5165 exists.
- Cross-client consistency: any new wetness/thermal scalar stays shared if built as (deterministic seed at load) + (step from shared inputs: live weather, season, wall clock) — the same contract snowAccum satisfies; using the offline random weather machine as an input breaks sharing (docs/INVARIANTS.md rule 6).

## Moorstead client — glint/cutout-flora shader machinery (src/mesher.js, src/floraLayer.js), post/grade pass (src/main.js), and fire FX (src/fire.js), assessed as grounding for rain-glisten/dew and heat-shimmer effects

### Findings
- Q1 GLINT MECHANISM — one shared uniform, one material, per-vertex gate. src/mesher.js:14-15: module-level `glintUniform = { uGlintTime }` + exported `setGlintTime(t)`. src/mesher.js:21-43 `addSnow(mat, key, glint)`: onBeforeCompile injects the snow wash into vertex/fragment; when `glint=true` (mesher.js:31-38) it additionally declares `attribute float aGlint` -> varying vGlint plus a position-hash varying vGlintH (mesher.js:33-35), and after `#include <color_fragment>` adds `float gl = vGlint * 0.12 * (0.5 + 0.5*sin(uGlintTime*2.0 + vGlintH)); diffuseColor.rgb += gl;` (mesher.js:38). The uniform OBJECT is assigned by reference into shader.uniforms (mesher.js:32), so one CPU write animates every mesh.
- Q1 WHICH MATERIALS: only `materials.cutout` is built with glint=true — src/mesher.js:63, cache key 'snow-cutout-glint' via customProgramCacheKey (mesher.js:41), MeshLambertMaterial, alphaTest 0.5, DoubleSide. `opaque` (mesher.js:62, key 'snow-opaque') has snow only; `liquid` (mesher.js:64-67) has the ice injection instead (addIce, mesher.js:45-57, uniform uFrozen + attribute aFreeze). The cache key is static per material, so there is exactly one compiled program for all cutout meshes.
- Q1 WHO TICKS IT: src/main.js:4791 — `this._glintT += dt; setGlintTime(this._glintT)` every frame in the in-game update loop (same block that drives setSnowLevel at main.js:4789 and setFrozen at main.js:4790).

Host-borne berry adornments (BILBERRY_FRUIT, BLACKBERRY, ROSEHIP, SLOE, ELDERBERRY, HAZELNUT — forage.js:14-21, routed through activeAdornments in src/flora-season.js:23-27) get aGlint=0 because glintTiles only includes scatter-forage + fruit tiles — NOTE this contradicts the tutorial copy at src/ui.js:600 ('glinting bilberry bush'); flag to James as a possible regression or stale copy.

Uniforms never create program variants; the established pattern is exactly this: module-level uniform object + setter (setSnowLevel mesher.js:16-20, setFrozen mesher.js:46) assigned into shader.uniforms inside the ONE onBeforeCompile with a static customProgramCacheKey. Adding `uniform float uWetness` to the glint injection and changing mesher.js:38 to e.g. `vGlint * (0.12 + uWetness * K) * ...` is a compile-time source edit to the single 'snow-cutout-glint' program — still one program, driven per frame like setGlintTime at main.js:4791. (b) Chunk cutout flora (heather/gorse/fern) — add an aGlint Float32BufferAttribute in GeoBuilder.build (mesher.js:117-129, alongside aSnowExp/aFreeze at :124-125) set per-block: nonzero for plant cutouts, 0 for TORCH/SIGNPOST/HOME_FLAG (the structural branch is already distinguished at mesher.js:168). An UN-gated uniform-only sparkle is wrong: the cutout material is shared by torches, signposts, flags and festival billboards (festivalKit.js:63, addBillboard festivalKit.js:54-74).
- Q3 GRADE-PASS INPUTS: GradeShader at src/main.js:110-136 — uniforms are ONLY tDiffuse + uTime (main.js:112); hash12 helper at main.js:120; fragment does grade/vignette/dither on vUv. uTime is ticked only under 'Fine' in renderFrame (main.js:333-340, tick at :335, `% 64`). Composer chain (setupComposer, main.js:296-308): RenderPass -> UnrealBloomPass -> OutputPass -> gradePass(ShaderPass). The grade pass runs AFTER OutputPass, i.e. in display sRGB space (comment main.js:106-109).
- Q3 DEPTH: there is NO sampleable depth anywhere in the composer chain — grep for DepthTexture/depthTexture across src/ hits only main.js:172, which is `this._cutoutDepth = new THREE.MeshDepthMaterial(...)` (main.js:172-174), the shadow-map alpha-tested depth material (used via customDepthMaterial in applyShadowFlags main.js:324), unrelated to post. EffectComposer's default targets have a depth renderbuffer, not a depth texture, so a shimmer mask in the grade pass is screen-space only today. Practical mask: a horizon band — but the camera pitches (camera.rotation.order 'YXZ', main.js:167), so the band should be centred on a CPU-computed uniform (e.g. uHorizonY = screen-space y of the world horizon, from camera pitch each frame in renderFrame, same place uTime ticks at main.js:335), optionally weighted by luminance from tDiffuse. To do it properly with depth you would construct the EffectComposer with a custom WebGLRenderTarget carrying a THREE.DepthTexture and pass it into GradeShader — plumbing that does not exist yet.
- Q3 DOME MIRAGE ALTERNATIVE: the sky dome is a ShaderMaterial on a 500-radius BackSide sphere, renderOrder -1, depthWrite:false (src/sky.js:108-151), with uTime + fbm/hash already in the fragment (sky.js:115, 128-132) and a horizon-gradient on dir.y (sky.js:134-136). A mirage there is easy (wobble bottomColor/cloud sampling where dir.y is near 0, driven by the existing uTime and a new heat uniform) BUT it is drawn first and terrain draws over it — it can only affect pixels where SKY is visible, i.e. a shimmering haze band above the skyline. It cannot displace/distort distant moor tops, buildings or the sea; true over-terrain shimmer must live in the post pass (Fine-only, since Plain bypasses the composer entirely at main.js:337-339).
- Q4 FIRE HEAT DISTORTION: nothing does refraction/distortion today. src/fire.js flames are additive billboard quads on ONE shared ShaderMaterial (getFlameMaterial, fire.js:184-198; billboard vertex fire.js:70-90), embers are GPU-side Points (fire.js:240-311), smoke is normal-blended quads (fire.js:317-389). None sample the framebuffer — no tDiffuse, no refraction, no grab-pass. REUSABLE PATTERNS: (1) the domain-warped fbm noise (hash/vnoise/fbm, fire.js:118-129; same idiom in sky dome sky.js:128-132) is precisely the displacement field a shimmer needs; (2) the single-clock tick — tickFires(t) at fire.js:517-527 driven once per frame from main.js:4780, plus registerFxMat/unregisterFxMat (fire.js:220-226) for any new uTime FX material; (3) the billboard vertex trick (fire.js:76-89) could host a per-fire 'haze quad' above flames, but as forward-rendered transparency it can only fake shimmer (brightness/alpha wobble), not bend the background; (4) for real per-fire distortion in the grade pass, FireLayer/hero-fire positions are known CPU-side (fireLayer update main.js:4776; hero registries fire.js:212-213) and could be projected to screen space and passed as a few vec3 uniforms (x, y, radius) forming the shimmer mask.

### Capabilities assessment
